Section A

New Information

Use present perfect to share something new or surprising

When something has happened, it is usually new information for the listener.
🤕Ow! I've cut my finger.tap for Thai

🚗The road is closed. There's been an accident.tap for Thai

🚔Police have arrested two men in connection with the robbery.tap for Thai

Section B · Special Focus

Gone to  vs  Been to

Same verb — very different meaning

🛫 has gone to

The person is still there — they have not returned.

🇮🇹James has gone to Italy.= He is there now.tap for Thai
James ไปอยู่ที่อิตาลีแล้ว= เขาอยู่ที่นั่นตอนนี้
🛬 has been to

The person visited and came back.

🏡Amy has been to Italy.= She visited and is home.tap for Thai
Amy เคยไปอิตาลีแล้ว= เธอไปมาแล้วและกลับบ้านแล้ว
🧪 Quick test: Can they answer the phone right now?  Yes → been to  |  No → gone to
Section C

Just · Already · Yet

Three adverbs that are very common with present perfect

● Just

A short time ago — something happened recently.

● Already

Sooner than expected — you don't need to remind me!

● Yet

Until now — used in questions & negatives. We expect it to happen.

🍱"Are you hungry?"  "No, I've just had lunch."tap for Thai
"คุณหิวไหม?"  "ไม่ ฉันเพิ่งทานข้าวกลางวัน""Are you hungry?"  "No, I've just had lunch."
💸"Don't forget to pay!"  "I've already paid it."tap for Thai
"อย่าลืมจ่ายนะ!"  "ฉันจ่ายไปแล้ว""Don't forget to pay!"  "I've already paid it."
🌧️Has it stopped raining yet?tap for Thai
ฝนหยุดตกแล้วหรือยัง?Has it stopped raining yet?
📧I've written the email, but I haven't sent it yet.tap for Thai
ฉันเขียนอีเมลแล้ว แต่ยังไม่ได้ส่งเลยI've written the email, but I haven't sent it yet.
Section D

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Both can be correct — the meaning is very similar in everyday speech

Present Perfect
🚪He's gone out.tap for Thai
เขาออกไปแล้วHe's gone out.
🍱I've just had lunch.tap for Thai
ฉันเพิ่งทานข้าวกลางวันI've just had lunch.
Past Simple
🚪He went out.tap for Thai
เขาออกไปข้างนอกHe went out.
🍱I just had lunch.tap for Thai
ฉันเพิ่งทานข้าวกลางวันI just had lunch.
💡 In British English, present perfect is more common. In American English, past simple is often used instead.

Summary

Everything on one slide

Subject + have / has + past participle = result NOW
🆕New information — use present perfect to tell someone something they don't know yet.
🔗Connection with NOW — the past action has a result we feel today.
